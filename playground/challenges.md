# SUBIT‑Lingua Playground — Challenges

These challenges are designed to push your structural intuition beyond basic
forms and into deeper pattern recognition. They are open‑ended, exploratory,
and intentionally difficult.

There are no correct answers.  
Only structure, coherence, and emergence.

---

# 1. Atomic & Doubled Forms

### Challenge 1.1 — Axis Inversion
Pick any doubled form (CV‑CV).  
Invert the bigrams (A↔U, E↔O).  
Interpret the transformation.

### Challenge 1.2 — Stability vs. Instability
Find:
- the most stable doubled form on each axis  
- the most unstable doubled form on each axis  

Explain your reasoning structurally.

### Challenge 1.3 — Directionality
Choose any axis.  
Arrange all 16 doubled forms into a cycle that feels like:
emergence → form → depth → energy → collapse → return.

---

# 2. Structured Forms (64‑cube)

### Challenge 2.1 — Cube Corners
Identify the 8 “corner” structured forms (A/A/A, A/A/U, …).  
Describe the personality of each corner.

### Challenge 2.2 — Pure Axial Shift
Pick a structured form.  
Shift only the WHO vowel.  
Interpret the resulting 4‑form sequence.

### Challenge 2.3 — Cross‑Diagonal
Pick a form.  
Invert all vowels (A↔U, E↔O).  
Interpret the cross‑diagonal relation.

---

# 3. Compounds (Chains)

### Challenge 3.1 — Two‑Unit Tension
Create a 2‑unit compound where the second unit:
- reinforces the first  
- contradicts the first  
- transforms the first  

Explain each case.

### Challenge 3.2 — Three‑Unit Narrative
Construct a 3‑unit chain that expresses:
- emergence → stabilization → release  
Interpret the chain structurally.

### Challenge 3.3 — Reversal
Take your chain from 3.2.  
Reverse the order.  
Compare the two narratives.

---

# 4. Transformations

### Challenge 4.1 — Axis Rotation Cycle
Pick any structured form.  
Apply K→T→M→K rotation three times.  
Observe the 3‑step cycle.

### Challenge 4.2 — Bigram Spiral
Pick any structured form.  
Rotate vowels A→E→O→U→A.  
Interpret the 4‑step spiral.

### Challenge 4.3 — Mixed Transformation
Pick any compound.  
Apply:
1. axis rotation to unit 1  
2. bigram rotation to unit 2  
3. position rotation to the whole chain  

Interpret the final result.
