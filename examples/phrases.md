# SUBIT‑Lingua — Example Phrases

This document provides **canonical example phrases** in SUBIT‑Lingua.  
All examples follow the minimal syntax rules:

- **[WHO] [WHERE] [WHEN]**  
- **Head + Modifier**  
- **Left‑to‑right composition**  

Each phrase is structurally interpretable and maps directly to SUBIT‑RFC.

---

# 1. Identity & Self‑Reference

| Phrase | Literal Structure | Meaning |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| **KA KA‑KA** | subject + selfhood | “I am myself.” |
| **KA KE** | subject + form | “I take form.” |
| **KA KO** | subject + source | “I originate.” |
| **KA KU** | subject + energy | “I act / I exert force.” |
| **KA‑TE‑ME KA** | structured role‑form + subject | “I am in my role.” |
