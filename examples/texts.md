# SUBIT‑Lingua — Example Texts

This document provides **complete example texts** written in SUBIT‑Lingua.  
Each text is followed by a structural English interpretation.

All examples follow the minimal syntax rules of SUBIT‑Lingua:

- [WHO] [WHERE] [WHEN]
- Head + Modifier
- Left‑to‑right composition
- No idioms, no irregularities

---

# 1. Text: Emergence of a Subject

**SUBIT‑Lingua**

KA‑TA‑MA  
KA‑TE‑ME  
KA‑TO‑MO  
KA‑TU‑MU  

**English Interpretation**

- KA‑TA‑MA — The subject emerges in open space.  
- KA‑TE‑ME — The subject takes structured form.  
- KA‑TO‑MO — The subject enters depth and origin.  
- KA‑TU‑MU — The subject reaches peak intensity.

**Meaning:**  
A subject appears, forms, deepens, and intensifies.
